Lemon and Parmesan Pasta

This is a recipe of my own, adapted from the cookbook Sicilia by Ben Tish. It makes for a great late night meal, and is pretty easy to throw together. It's essentially my quicker version for when I don't have the energy to cook. By the way, you can totally make it with leftover pasta, I do all the time.

  • Time:It's a race. Maybe 10-15, not counting the time it takes to cook the pasta.
  • Serves: As much as you want.
Ingredients Quantity
Pasta of your choice (I reccomend something like Sagnarelli As much as you want to eat
Olive Oil A drizzle, added as needed
Breadcrumbs A little sprinkle (as much as you want, but don't go to heavy)
Garlic A spoon (as much as you want)
Lemon Juice A decent amount. Depends on how lemony you want it.
Parmesan, grated A hefty amount
Salt, pepper, red pepper To taste

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta and set aside. Save some of the pasta water for later.
  2. In a medium sized pan, start heating up some olive oil. Then, add the breadcrumbs. In an ideal world, you would lightly toast it. If you're in a rush, cook it until your patience runs out because you're too hungry.
  3. Add garlic. Then add in cooked pasta and lemon juice. Cook for like a minute or so.
  4. Remove the pan from heat. Add in a splash on the reserved pasta water. Start mixing in the cheese, a bit at a time, until you're happy with it.
  5. Add any additional seasonings you'd like. Enjoy!

Pastina

This is a fabulous meal if you are feeling a bit under the weather, or just want something nice and warm and hearty! Also, this probably isn't the traditional way to make it, but it's the way I usually make it because I don't know that way.
  • Time:30 minutes? I never checked.
  • Serves: About one decently large bowl
Ingredients Quantity
Pastina 1/4 Cup
Celery Two stalks
White Onion 1/2 an onion
Garlic As much as you want
Water 2 cups
Vegetable (or your choice of flavour) Buillon
**Alternative:substitute water and buillon with 2 cups broth of your choice 		2 tbs
Butter (optional, can also be substituted with olive oil) Like a spoon or two
Parmesan, grated The more the merrier!
Salt, pepper, red pepper (optional) To taste!

Instructions

  1. Chop the celery and onion (and the garlic if you're using fresh garlic. I never do). Dice the onion and cut the celery in the normal way to cut it.
  2. In a medium sized pot, melt a bit of butter, and throw in the garlic. Cook that for a tiny bit, and then add the celery and onion. Cook until it looks good (you don't need to fully cook it because it will cook in the broth).
  3. Add two cups of water to the pot, and bring to a boil.
  4. When the water boils, stir in the buillon. If you just used broth to start out with, skip this step.
  5. Add in the pastina. Bring down the heat a bit, and cover
  6. Cook the pastina until the liquid has been largely absorbed, about 6-8 minutes. Stir when you remember to.
  7. Remove the pot from heat. Stir in parmesan, and any additional butter or olive oil you wanted to add. Then, season to taste.
  8. Serve in a bowl, and add a little parmesan on top! Enjoy!
